# EU AI Act Risk Self-Assessment Tool

**[Live demo →](https://eu-ai-act-risk-tool.streamlit.app)** &nbsp;|&nbsp; Built for [Tolt Innovations](https://www.tolt.ie)

---

## What this does

A compliance officer at an Irish financial services firm answers ~15 questions about an AI system they plan to deploy. The tool classifies it under the EU AI Act risk framework (Prohibited / High-risk / Limited / Minimal), lists every obligation triggered with article citations, estimates maximum penalty exposure, and — for CBI-regulated firms — overlays the Central Bank of Ireland's 2026 supervisory expectations. Output includes a downloadable, branded PDF report.

---

## Why it exists

The European Commission published 148 pages of draft guidelines on Article 6 classification on **19 May 2026**, with stakeholder consultation open until 23 June 2026. Most Irish financial services firms do not yet have a fast, defensible first-pass screen to determine which of their AI systems are high-risk under the Act.

The Central Bank of Ireland's 2026 Regulatory & Supervisory Outlook elevates AI as a supervisory priority, holding firms fully accountable for outcomes generated by AI systems — including those operated by third parties. Tolt Innovations' Compliance Hub addresses exactly this gap.

This tool is the first-pass intake screen that sits upstream of that Compliance Hub.

## How the classification works

The classifier follows the EU AI Act decision tree exactly:

```
1. Article 5 check
   → Any prohibited practice? → PROHIBITED (max penalty 7% / €35M)

2. Article 6(1) check
   → Safety component of an Annex I regulated product?
   → AND requires third-party conformity assessment?
   → Both YES → HIGH-RISK

3. Article 6(2) check
   → Does intended purpose fall under any Annex III area?
   → (biometrics, critical infrastructure, education, employment,
      essential services, law enforcement, migration, justice)
   → If NO → proceed to default

4. Article 6(3) filter (only if step 3 hit)
   → Does the system meet any narrow-task condition?
     (a) narrow procedural task only
     (b) improves result of prior completed human activity
     (c) detects patterns/deviations without replacing human assessment
     (d) preparatory task only
   → BLOCKER: profiling of natural persons disables ALL filter conditions
   → Filter applies AND no profiling → LIMITED (with Art 6(4) docs + Art 49(2) registration)
   → Filter does not apply → HIGH-RISK confirmed

5. Default → MINIMAL risk
   → Article 50 transparency obligations apply if system interacts with humans
```

Every branch of the classifier cites the relevant EU AI Act article inline as a code comment. The classifier is intentionally free of UI dependencies — it can be imported into any application, including a FastAPI service.

## Architecture

```
eu-ai-act-risk-tool/
├── app.py                  Streamlit UI — no business logic
├── risk_classifier.py      Core decision tree — typed, tested, no UI imports
├── obligations.py          Risk tier → obligations + penalty reference data
├── report_generator.py     ReportLab PDF generation
├── data/
│   └── annex_iii.json      All 8 Annex III areas with use cases, structured
├── tests/
│   └── test_classifier.py  8 unit tests covering edge cases
└── requirements.txt
```

`risk_classifier.py` is the heart of the system. It takes a fully typed `AssessmentInput` dataclass and returns a fully typed `AssessmentResult`. Zero Streamlit imports. Zero PDF imports. It could be wrapped in a FastAPI endpoint and called from any front-end.

## What I'd build next

- **Article 6(3) filter walkthrough** as a guided decision UI rather than a flat checklist — the Commission's May 2026 guidelines make this nuanced enough to warrant multi-step guidance
- **Persistent AI register** — a client-level inventory of all AI systems assessed, storing tier, pathway, obligations, and assessment date (the "Evidence Layer" Tolt references on their site)
- **CBI-specific reporting templates** — pre-formatted outputs matching Central Bank of Ireland model risk governance documentation requirements
- **Live regulation sync** — once the Commission finalises the Article 6 guidelines after the 23 June 2026 consultation, update the decision tree automatically via a reference JSON rather than requiring a code change
- **White-label per client** — parameterise the branding so the tool can be deployed as a self-service portal for each Tolt client, capturing their AI inventory as structured data
